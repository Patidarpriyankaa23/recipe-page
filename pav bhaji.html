Pav Bhaji

A popular Mumbai street food dish of mashed vegetables cooked with aromatic spices, served hot with butter-toasted pav (bread rolls).

Prep Time: 15 min

Cook Time: 25 min

Serves: 4

Ingredients

  • 2 potatoes (boiled & mashed)
  • 1 cup cauliflower (finely chopped)
  • 1/2 cup green peas
  • 1 green capsicum (chopped)
  • 2 onions (finely chopped)
  • 2 tomatoes (finely chopped)
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tbsp Pav Bhaji masala
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • Fresh coriander leaves
  • 8 pav buns
  • Extra butter for toasting

Instructions

  1. Heat butter in a pan, sauté chopped onions until golden.
  2. Add ginger-garlic paste, then tomatoes. Cook until soft.
  3. Add capsicum, peas, cauliflower, and mashed potatoes.
  4. Mix in turmeric, chili powder, pav bhaji masala, and salt. Cook for 10-15 mins while mashing the veggies.
  5. Add water to adjust consistency. Simmer for 5 mins.
  6. Garnish with butter and chopped coriander.
  7. Slit pav buns, toast on a tawa with butter until golden.
  8. Serve bhaji hot with toasted pav, chopped onions, and lemon wedges.

Nutrition (Per Serving)

  • Calories: 320
  • Fat: 15g
  • Carbs: 38g
  • Protein: 8g

Pro Tips

  • Use Amul butter for authentic street-style taste.
  • Adjust spice level to your liking.
  • Toast pav just before serving to keep them crisp.
