Rabdi Malpua

A festive Indian dessert of deep-fried pancakes soaked in syrup and topped with rich, creamy rabdi and nuts.

Prep Time: 20 min

Cook Time: 40 min

Serves: 6-8

Ingredients

For Malpua Batter:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 2 tbsp semolina (sooji)
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1/4 cup mashed ripe banana (optional)
  • 1/2 tsp fennel seeds
  • A pinch of cardamom powder

For Sugar Syrup:

  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup water
  • A few saffron strands
  • 1/4 tsp cardamom powder

For Rabdi:

  • 1 liter full cream milk
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • Chopped pistachios and almonds
  • 1/4 tsp cardamom powder
  • Saffron strands

Other:

  • Ghee or oil for frying

Instructions

  1. Make Rabdi: Boil milk in a wide pan and simmer on low flame until it reduces to 1/3rd. Add sugar, saffron, cardamom, and dry fruits. Cool and chill.
  2. Prepare Batter: Mix flour, semolina, banana (if using), milk, fennel, and cardamom to a smooth batter. Let it rest for 30 minutes.
  3. Make Syrup: Boil sugar, water, saffron, and cardamom until slightly sticky. Keep warm.
  4. Fry Malpua: Heat ghee and pour ladles of batter to form small pancakes. Fry until golden and crisp on both sides.
  5. Dip each malpua in warm sugar syrup for 1–2 mins.
  6. Place malpua on a plate and top with a generous spoon of chilled rabdi.
  7. Garnish with chopped dry fruits and serve warm or chilled.

Nutrition (Per Serving)

  • Calories: 350
  • Fat: 18g
  • Carbs: 38g
  • Protein: 6g

Pro Tips

  • Use full-fat milk for creamy rabdi.
  • Do not over-soak malpuas in syrup.
  • Rabdi can be made a day ahead and stored in the fridge.
