Dal Bati

A traditional Rajasthani dish of baked wheat flour balls served with spiced mixed lentils, ghee, and churma.

Prep Time: 30 min

Cook Time: 1 hr

Serves: 4

Ingredients

For Bati:

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour
  • 1/4 cup semolina (sooji)
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/4 cup ghee
  • Water as needed

For Dal:

  • 1/2 cup toor dal
  • 1/4 cup chana dal
  • 1/4 cup moong dal
  • 1 onion (chopped)
  • 1 tomato (chopped)
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp ghee
  • Coriander for garnish

Instructions

  1. Make the Bati: Mix flour, semolina, baking soda, salt, and ghee. Add water to make a stiff dough. Shape into balls and bake at 180°C for 25–30 min or until golden. Dip in melted ghee before serving.
  2. Cook the Dal: Wash and pressure-cook all dals together with turmeric and salt. In a pan, heat ghee, add cumin, onion, ginger-garlic, and sauté. Add tomatoes and spices. Add cooked dal and simmer for 10 minutes.
  3. Serve: Serve hot bati with dal, ghee on top, and churma or salad on the side.

Nutrition (Per Serving)

  • Calories: 450
  • Protein: 15g
  • Fat: 20g
  • Carbs: 50g

Pro Tips

  • Bake batis until they are crisp outside but soft inside.
  • Use ghee generously for authentic flavor.
  • You can also deep-fry the batis for a traditional version.
